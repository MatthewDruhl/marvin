## What Iterable Does

AI-powered cross-channel customer engagement platform. Marketers use it to orchestrate campaigns across email, SMS, push notifications, in-app messages, and web push — all from one platform.

### Key Product Features
- **Journeys / Studio** — drag-and-drop visual workflow builder for multi-step campaigns
- **AI Suite (Iterable Nova)** — send-time optimization, channel selection, content personalization
- **Experiments** — A/B testing across any campaign dimension
- **Messaging Insights** — aggregate campaign performance analytics
- **Real-time data ingestion** — SDKs, APIs, data warehouse integrations
- **MCP Server** — recently launched (relevant to AI/tooling background)

### Company Stats

| **Iterable** | Large enterprises needing flexible workflows + strong support |
| **Braze** | Mobile-first brands, real-time at scale |
| **Klaviyo** | SMB/ecommerce (esp. Shopify) |
| **Salesforce Marketing Cloud** | Salesforce ecosystem shops |

### Iterable's Market Position
Iterable differentiates on **easier implementation** vs Braze but requires **more engineering resources** than simpler tools. The product's flexibility (custom data models, API integrations, workflow logic) means engineering teams are heavily involved in setup, maintenance, and scaling.

This complexity creates internal engineering operations challenges — which is exactly what this TPM role exists to manage. When your product *requires* engineering resources to deliver customer value, you need someone ensuring:
- Engineering teams aren't drowning in operational toil vs. building features
- Cross-team dependencies (platform, product, data, SRE) don't create bottlenecks
- Developer experience is good enough that engineers are productive
- Visibility into engineering health — where are teams blocked, what's slowing delivery

## Interview Process (Typical)

1. **Recruiter call** (20-30 min) — background, fit, motivations
2. **Technical screening** (1-2 remote rounds) — Zoom + Coderpad, medium-difficulty problems
3. **Virtual onsite** — multiple blocks (30-60 min each) with devs and EMs
4. Possibly a **case study** — analyze data/problem, present recommendations
- Average process duration: ~23 days
