Texas LLC · Training Start Q1 2027

## 01 Business Overview

A mobile RV technician business offers on-site diagnosis, repair, and preventative maintenance to RV owners at campgrounds, storage facilities, and residences — eliminating the need to tow to a dealer. Demand is strong, supply of skilled techs is thin, and overhead is low.

Mission: To provide certified, professional, mobile RV maintenance and repair services — saving owners time and money while keeping their rigs road-ready and campsite-ready.

### Market Opportunity

- 11+ million RVs on US roads with a chronic shortage of certified technicians
- Mobile service commands a premium — customers pay gladly to avoid dealer wait times of weeks or months
- Revenue from diagnostic fees, hourly labor ($85–$150/hr), and parts markup
- Revenue supplemented by disability income during ramp-up period

Contact: (903) 386-0444 · nrvta.com

Target: Call Q3–Q4 2026 to confirm Q1 2027 cohort dates, get current tuition, and reserve your seat.

Note on the Klein CL810 multimeter: must have AC AND DC amp clamp capability. Do not substitute — students who bring other brands often end up buying this one mid-course.

### RVTAA Certification Ladder

Tier 1 — Registered RV Service Technician (1 week / 48 hrs)
- RVTF 200 — RV Fundamentals (40 hrs: DC/AC electrical, propane, plumbing, refrigerator, AC, water heater, furnace)
- RVTP 900 — Registered Exam Review Day (8 hrs)
- Included as Week 1 of the Tier 2 program — no need to take separately

Tier 2 — Advanced RV Service Technician / Certified (5 weeks / 188 hrs) — PRIMARY TARGET
- RVTF 200 — RV Fundamentals (40 hrs, Week 1)
- RVTA 301 — Air Conditioners & Heat Pumps: Dometic/Coleman, wiring diagrams, live troubleshooting (40 hrs: 16 lecture / 24 lab)
- RVTE 301 — Exterior Systems: slide-outs, leveling, roofs, sidewalls, axles, suspension (40 hrs: 16 lecture / 24 lab)
- RVTR 301 — Absorption Refrigerators: Dometic/Norcold, propane & electrical circuits, circuit boards (40 hrs: 16 lecture / 24 lab)
- RVTW 301 — Water Heaters & Furnaces: Suburban/Dometic/Atwood, gas & electric test points (40 hrs: 16 lecture / 24 lab)
- RVTP 910 — Certified Exam Review Day (8 hrs)
- Credential: RVTAA Certified RV Service Technician

## 03 Legal & Insurance

### Texas LLC Formation

- File Articles of Organization with Texas Secretary of State — $300 filing fee
- No state income tax — TX is a strong business domicile
- Registered Agent: use yourself at a TX address or a registered agent service (~$50–$100/yr)
- Obtain EIN at IRS.gov — free, takes 10 minutes, do this immediately after LLC approval
- Open dedicated business bank account — keep business and personal finances completely separate
- Draft Operating Agreement — even as single-member LLC ($50–$100 via LegalZoom or attorney)
- Sales tax on parts across states — defer to CPA

### Insurance

Do not take any paying jobs until General Liability and Garage Keepers coverage is in place.

| Policy | Cost Estimate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| General Liability ($1M) | $500–$1,500/yr | Required by most campgrounds to operate on site |
| Garage Keepers / Care Custody Control | $400–$1,000/yr | Covers damage to customer's RV while in your care |
| Commercial Auto | $1,200–$2,400/yr | Personal policy will NOT cover business use |
| Tools & Equipment Floater | $200–$500/yr | Covers loss or damage to your tool loadout |
| Pollution / Completed Operations | Ask your agent | LP gas liability after job completion |
| Workers' Comp | Required if/when you hire | Not needed as solo operator in most TX situations |

Carriers to contact: Progressive Commercial, Markel, Philadelphia Insurance, RLI. Use a local independent agent who handles contractors.

### Accounting

- Software: QuickBooks Self-Employed or Simple Start (~$15–$35/mo) — track income, expenses, mileage
- CPA: find one familiar with LLC / self-employed clients — ask about Section 179 (tools), mileage deduction, quarterly estimated taxes
- Estimated CPA cost: $300–$700/yr for basic filings
- S-Corp election: discuss with CPA once net profit exceeds ~$50K/yr — reduces self-employment taxes

## 07 Operations & Systems

### Day-to-Day

- Scheduling / Invoicing: Jobber (~$49–$149/mo) or Housecall Pro — purpose-built for mobile service
- Payment: Square or Stripe; offer Zelle as backup — invoice the moment the job is done
- Work Order: have every customer sign before touching their rig — include scope, labor rate, pre-existing condition disclaimer, payment terms
- Documentation: photo every job before and after — protects against disputes and feeds marketing content
- Service Radius: define a firm radius (30–50 miles typical); charge trip fee for outliers

### Parts Sourcing

- Camping World Pro trade program
- Lippert (LCI) dealer portal
- Dometic and Norcold direct
- Carefree of Colorado
- Local NAPA or Carquest for hardware

### Job Workflow

1. Intake call / booking
2. Send confirmation + work order for signature
3. Diagnose on-site
4. Provide written estimate
5. Get customer approval
6. Complete work
7. Photo document before and after
8. Collect payment on-site
9. Request Google review

### Continuing Education

- RVTAA certification renewal every 2–4 years
- Budget ~$200–$400/yr for renewals and new system training (lithium, solar, TPMS)
- OEM manufacturer training portals (Lippert, Dometic, etc.) — often free, builds brand expertise
