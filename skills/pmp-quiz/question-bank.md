## Section 4: Project Management Terms

### Project Bosses (Sponsor, Program Manager)

**Q1 - T/F:** The project sponsor is responsible for funding the project and making key decisions.
**A:** True. The sponsor is the project champion — they provide funding, make high-level decisions, and authorize the project charter.

**Q2 - MC:** Who is typically senior to the project manager and may resolve conflicts between projects?
A) The project sponsor
B) The program manager
C) The functional manager
D) The stakeholder
**A:** B) The program manager. They oversee multiple related projects and resolve conflicts between them.

**Q3 - T/F:** The project sponsor is always someone internal to the organization.
**A:** False. The sponsor can be internal or external to the organization.

### Product vs Project Management

**Q1 - MC:** What is the key difference between product management and project management?
A) Product management focuses on execution; project management focuses on strategy
B) Product management encompasses the full lifecycle of a product; project management focuses on delivering specific projects within scope, time, and budget
C) Project managers are senior to product managers
D) Product management only applies to software development
**A:** B) Product management covers strategic planning, development, and ongoing lifecycle. Project management focuses on delivering specific projects on time, within budget, to quality standards.

**Q2 - T/F:** Project management is one component of product management, not the other way around.
**A:** True. While project management focuses on execution and delivery, it is just one part of the broader product management lifecycle.

**Q2 - T/F:** "Communications" as an area of a project means ensuring all stakeholders get the correct information at the right time.
**A:** True.

**Q3 - MC:** Which project area focuses on acquiring resources from outside the project team?
A) Resources
B) Procurements
C) Stakeholder Engagement
D) Communications
**A:** B) Procurements — acquiring resources from outside the project team.

### PM Approaches: Predictive vs Adaptive

**Q1 - MC:** Which approach involves extensive upfront planning, sequential execution, and limited flexibility for changes?
A) Adaptive
B) Hybrid
C) Predictive
D) Iterative
**A:** C) Predictive (also called traditional or waterfall). It emphasizes control, documentation, and a well-defined change control process.

**Q2 - T/F:** The adaptive approach embraces change and emphasizes iterative, incremental development.
**A:** True. Adaptive (agile) approaches like Scrum and Kanban focus on flexibility, collaboration, and continuous feedback.

**Q3 - MC:** Which of the following is a characteristic of the adaptive approach?
A) Emphasis on control and documentation
B) Sequential execution
C) Self-organizing teams
D) Limited changes with a defined change control process
**A:** C) Self-organizing teams. Adaptive approaches also emphasize customer collaboration, iterative development, and adaptability.

### Organizational Structures

**Q1 - MC:** In which organizational structure does the project manager have the MOST authority?
A) Functional
B) Weak matrix
C) Strong matrix
D) Projectized
**A:** D) Projectized. The PM has high/total authority, full-time team, and controls the budget.

**Q2 - T/F:** In a functional organization, the project manager has little to no authority over resources.
**A:** True. In a functional structure, team members report to the functional manager. The PM has little/no authority and resource availability is low.

**Q3 - MC:** In a balanced matrix organization, who controls the budget?
A) The project manager
B) The functional manager
C) It's mixed between PM and functional manager
D) The project sponsor
**A:** C) Mixed. In a balanced matrix, authority and budget control are shared between the PM and functional manager.

**Q4 - T/F:** In a weak matrix, the PM's role is part-time.
**A:** True. In both functional and weak matrix structures, the PM role is part-time (PT).

### Risks vs Issues vs Assumptions vs Constraints

**Q1 - MC:** What is the difference between a risk and an issue?
A) Risks are positive; issues are negative
B) Risks are potential future events; issues are current problems
C) Risks affect cost; issues affect schedule
D) There is no difference
**A:** B) Risks are potential events that MAY occur in the future. Issues are problems that have already arisen and ARE currently affecting the project.

**Q2 - T/F:** Assumptions are statements believed to be true for planning purposes, even without proof.
**A:** True. Assumptions are considered true or valid for planning and decision-making, though they carry inherent risk if wrong.

**Q3 - MC:** Which of the following is an example of a constraint?
A) "We assume the vendor will deliver on time"
B) "There's a 30% chance of a supply chain delay"
C) "The budget cannot exceed $500,000"
D) "The server crashed during testing"
**A:** C) Constraints are limitations or restrictions. A is an assumption, B is a risk, D is an issue.

### Project Constraints (6 Constraints)

**Q1 - MC:** Which of the following are the six project constraints?
A) Scope, Schedule, Cost, Risk, Quality, Resources
B) Scope, Schedule, Cost, Quality, People, Procurement
C) Time, Money, People, Risk, Scope, Communication
D) Scope, Schedule, Budget, Stakeholders, Quality, Risk
**A:** A) Scope, Schedule, Cost, Risk, Quality, Resources — often visualized as a hexagon.

**Q2 - T/F:** Changing one project constraint typically affects one or more of the other constraints.
**A:** True. The constraints are interconnected — expanding scope typically impacts schedule, cost, or both.

A) Relationship building
B) Budget forecasting
C) Communication and conflict management
D) Motivation and influence
**A:** B) Budget forecasting. EQ supports relationship building, communication, conflict management, motivation, influence, leadership, decision making, and stakeholder management.

**Q2 - T/F:** Emotional Intelligence is the ability to recognize, understand, and manage emotions in oneself and others.

### Leadership vs Management

**Q1 - MC:** Which statement BEST distinguishes leadership from management?
A) Leaders focus on tasks; managers focus on people
B) Leaders inspire and set direction; managers plan, organize, and control
C) Leaders have formal authority; managers have informal influence
D) Leaders focus on short-term goals; managers focus on long-term vision
**A:** B) Leadership focuses on inspiring, influencing, and setting direction. Management focuses on planning, organizing, coordinating, and controlling resources for efficient execution.

**Q2 - T/F:** According to PMI, "Leadership is different than authority."
**A:** True. Authority is the right to exercise power and control. Leadership is about motivating people toward a common goal — it doesn't require formal authority.

**Q3 - MC:** According to PMI's comparison table, managers "do things right" while leaders:
A) Do the right things
B) Focus on the bottom line
C) Maintain stability
D) Rely on control
**A:** A) "Do the right things." Managers do things right (efficiency/process); leaders do the right things (effectiveness/direction).

## Section 5: Project Management Principles

### PMI Code of Ethics

**Q1 - MC:** The PMI Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct is based on which four values?
A) Integrity, Trust, Accountability, Excellence
B) Responsibility, Respect, Fairness, Honesty
C) Leadership, Stewardship, Service, Quality
D) Ethics, Compliance, Transparency, Courage
**A:** B) Responsibility, Respect, Fairness, Honesty.

**Q2 - T/F:** The PMI Code of Ethics is based on principles, which can but do not necessarily reflect morals.
**A:** True. Principles serve as foundational guidelines. A code of ethics is related to morals and establishes expectations for moral conduct.

### 12 Principles of Project Management

**Q1 - MC:** How many principles of project management are defined in PMBOK 7?
A) 8
B) 10
C) 12
D) 14
**A:** C) 12 principles.

**Q2 - T/F:** "Focus on value" is one of the 12 principles of project management.
**A:** True. The 12 principles are: (1) Stewardship, (2) Collaborative team environment, (3) Stakeholder engagement, (4) Focus on value, (5) Systems thinking, (6) Leadership, (7) Tailoring, (8) Quality, (9) Complexity, (10) Risk, (11) Adaptability and resiliency, (12) Change.

**Q3 - MC:** Which principle states that project teams should use "just enough" process to accomplish the desired outcome?
A) Navigate complexity
B) Tailor based on context
C) Build quality into processes
D) Embrace adaptability
**A:** B) Tailor based on context. Each project is unique, and tailoring the approach produces benefits like deeper commitment, reduced waste, and more efficient resource use.

B) Care
C) Profitability

**Q2 - T/F:** A holistic view of stewardship considers financial, social, technical, and sustainable environmental awareness.

### Collaborative Team Environment

**Q1 - MC:** What is the difference between accountability and responsibility?
A) They mean the same thing
B) Accountability can be shared; responsibility cannot
C) Accountability is being answerable for an outcome and cannot be shared; responsibility is being obligated to do something and can be shared
D) Responsibility is being answerable; accountability is being obligated
**A:** C) Accountability = answerable for outcome (not shared). Responsibility = obligated to do/fulfill (can be shared).

**Q2 - T/F:** Authority is the right to make relevant decisions, establish or improve procedures, apply project resources, and give approvals.
**A:** True.

### Stakeholder Engagement

**Q1 - MC:** Stakeholder engagement should be:
A) Reactive — respond when stakeholders raise concerns
B) Proactive — engage from start to end of the project
C) Limited to key stakeholders only
D) Handled exclusively by the project sponsor
**A:** B) Proactive. Engage stakeholders proactively from start to finish to contribute to project success and customer satisfaction.

**Q2 - T/F:** Stakeholders can affect scope, schedule, cost, project team, plans, outcomes, culture, benefits realization, risk, quality, and success.
**A:** True. Stakeholders can impact virtually every aspect of a project.

### Focus on Value

**Q1 - T/F:** Value is the ultimate indicator of project success.
**A:** True.

**Q2 - MC:** When can value be realized in a project?
A) Only at the end of the project
B) Only after the project is complete
C) Throughout the project, at the end, or after completion
D) Only during the planning phase
**A:** C) Value can be realized throughout the project, at the end, or after the project is complete.

**Q3 - T/F:** Value can only be defined in quantitative (monetary) terms.
**A:** False. Value and the benefits that contribute to it can be defined in quantitative AND/OR qualitative terms.

### Systems Thinking

**Q1 - MC:** Systems thinking in project management means:
A) Using software systems to manage the project
B) Taking a holistic view of how project parts interact with each other and external systems
C) Following a systematic step-by-step process
D) Implementing IT systems for the project
**A:** B) Systems thinking means recognizing that a project is a system of interdependent and interacting domains of activity, and viewing it holistically.

**Q2 - T/F:** A project exists within larger systems, and a project deliverable may become part of a larger system to realize benefits.
**A:** True.

### Leadership Behaviors

**Q1 - T/F:** Only the project manager can demonstrate leadership on a project.
**A:** False. Any project team member can demonstrate leadership behaviors, regardless of role or position.

**Q2 - MC:** Which statement about leadership is correct according to PMI?
A) Leadership requires formal authority
B) Effective leaders use the same style for all situations
C) Effective leaders adapt their style to the situation
D) Leadership and authority are the same thing
**A:** C) Effective leaders adapt their style to the situation. Leadership is different from authority, and leaders recognize differences in motivation among team members.

### Tailoring Based on Context

**Q1 - T/F:** A tailored project approach can produce benefits such as deeper commitment from team members and reduced waste.
**A:** True. Other benefits include customer-oriented focus and more efficient use of resources.

**Q2 - MC:** Why should project teams tailor their approach?
A) To follow PMI standards exactly as written
B) Because each project is unique and requires methods suited to its context
C) To reduce documentation requirements
D) Because stakeholders always demand customization
**A:** B) Each project is unique. Tailoring the development methods to suit the project's objectives, stakeholders, governance, and environment leads to better outcomes.

### Build Quality into Processes and Deliverables

**Q1 - MC:** Quality in project management is primarily about:
A) Exceeding all stakeholder expectations
B) Meeting the acceptance criteria for deliverables
C) Having zero defects in all deliverables
D) Spending the maximum budget on quality assurance
**A:** B) Meeting the acceptance criteria for deliverables. Quality is about satisfying stakeholder expectations and fulfilling project and product requirements.

**Q2 - T/F:** Quality dimensions include performance, conformity, reliability, resilience, satisfaction, efficiency, and sustainability.
**A:** True.

### Navigate Complexity

**Q1 - MC:** Which of the following is NOT a common source of project complexity?
A) Human behavior
B) System behavior
C) Project budget size
D) Uncertainty and ambiguity
**A:** C) Project budget size. The common sources are: human behavior, system behavior, uncertainty and ambiguity, and technological innovation.

**Q2 - T/F:** Complexity can arise at any point during the project and is often the result of many conditions and events.
**A:** True. Teams often cannot forecast complexity emerging because it results from many interacting conditions.

---

### Optimize Risk Responses

**Q1 - T/F:** Risks can only be negative (threats) to a project.
**A:** False. Risks can be positive (opportunities) or negative (threats). Teams seek to maximize opportunities and decrease exposure to threats.

**Q2 - MC:** Risk responses should be all of the following EXCEPT:
A) Appropriate for the significance of the risk
B) Cost effective
C) Eliminated entirely before the project starts
D) Owned by a responsible person
**A:** C) Risks cannot all be eliminated before the project starts. Responses should be appropriate, cost-effective, realistic, agreed to by stakeholders, and owned by a responsible person.

### Embrace Adaptability and Resiliency

**Q1 - MC:** What is the difference between adaptability and resiliency?
A) They mean the same thing
B) Adaptability is responding to changing conditions; resiliency is absorbing impacts and recovering from setbacks
C) Adaptability is for agile projects only; resiliency is for predictive projects
D) Resiliency is proactive; adaptability is reactive
**A:** B) Adaptability = ability to respond to changing conditions. Resiliency = ability to absorb impacts and recover quickly from setbacks or failures.

**Q2 - T/F:** A project rarely performs exactly as initially planned.
**A:** True. Projects are influenced by internal and external factors — new requirements, issues, stakeholder influences — which exist in a system of interactions.

### Enable Change

**Q1 - MC:** Effective change management uses which approach?
A) A forceful strategy to ensure compliance
B) A motivational strategy rather than a forceful one
C) Avoiding change altogether
D) Implementing all changes immediately without stakeholder input
**A:** B) A motivational strategy rather than a forceful one. Stakeholder engagement and motivational approaches help embrace change.

**Q2 - T/F:** Change can originate from internal sources, external sources, or both.
**A:** True.

**Q3 - T/F:** Attempting too much change in a short time can lead to change fatigue and/or resistance.
**A:** True. This is why stakeholder engagement and a structured approach are important.

**Q3 - MC:** Which process group authorizes the start of the project and assigns the project manager?

B) Executing

**Q4 - MC:** Which process group tracks, reviews, and regulates project progress and looks for areas where changes to the plan are required?
A) Planning
B) Executing
C) Closing

**A:** D) Monitoring & Controlling.

**Q5 - MC:** The Closing process group is done to:
A) Define the scope of the project

### Common Inputs

**Q13 - MC:** Which of the following is NOT one of the 4 common inputs to most processes?

B) Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEF)
C) Organizational Process Assets (OPA)
D) Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
**A:** D) WBS. The 4 common inputs are: Project Management Plan, EEF, OPA, and Project Documents.

### Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEF)

**Q14 - MC:** Enterprise Environmental Factors (EEF) are best described as:
A) Documents created during the project
B) Things that impact the project but are not part of the project itself
C) Historical records from past projects
D) The project manager's personal preferences
**A:** B) Things that impact the project but are not part of the project itself. They can enhance or constrain project management options.

**Q15 - MC:** Which of the following is an EXTERNAL Enterprise Environmental Factor?
A) Organizational culture
B) Resource availability
C) Government or industry standards
D) Information technology software
**A:** C) Government or industry standards. Other external EEFs include political climate, legal restrictions, commercial databases, and financial considerations.

**Q16 - MC:** Which of the following is an INTERNAL Enterprise Environmental Factor?
A) Legal restrictions
B) Political climate
C) Organizational culture
D) Commercial databases
**A:** C) Organizational culture. Other internal EEFs include organizational structure, risk appetite, communication channels, IT software, infrastructure, resource availability, and employee capability.

A) External factors that constrain the project
B) Information, policies, procedures, documents, or knowledge bases used to help achieve objectives
C) The project manager's personal tools
D) Government regulations that apply to the project
**A:** B) Information, policies, procedures, documents, or knowledge bases that help achieve objectives. They are kept in a central repository and updated throughout the project.

**Q18 - MC:** Which of the following is an example of an OPA?
A) Government regulations
B) Political climate
C) Historical information and lessons learned
D) Market conditions
**A:** C) Historical information and lessons learned. Other OPA examples include project templates, software tools, project closure guidelines, risk/change control procedures, and issue/defect management procedures.

## Section 8: The 49 Processes — Initiating

### Develop Project Charter (Video 87)

**Q1 - [SCENARIO]:** You are a new PM and your sponsor asks what must happen before you can assign team members and spend organizational resources on your project. What do you tell them?
A) Complete the project management plan
B) Get the project charter approved
C) Create the WBS
D) Identify all stakeholders
**A:** B) The approved project charter formally authorizes the project and gives the PM authority to consume organizational resources. Without it, you cannot begin. *(Video 87)*

**Q2 - MC:** Which TWO business documents are key inputs to the Develop Project Charter process?
A) Business case and benefits management plan
B) Scope statement and WBS
C) Risk register and stakeholder register
D) Project management plan and change log
**A:** A) The business case justifies the investment, and the benefits management plan describes measurable benefits the project will produce once complete. *(Video 87)*

**Q3 - [SCENARIO]:** A stakeholder asks why your project should be funded over three competing projects. Which document should you reference?
A) The project management plan
B) The business case
C) The assumption log
D) The stakeholder register
**A:** B) The business case contains the justification — market demand, customer requests, legal requirements, ROI — proving the project is worth the investment. *(Video 87)*

**Q4 - MC:** What level of detail does the project charter contain?
A) Very detailed scope, schedule, and budget
B) High-level scope, high-level budget, high-level schedule, and high-level risks
C) Only the project manager's name and authority level
D) Detailed work packages and activity lists
**A:** B) The charter is high-level. It does not contain detailed specifications — those come later in planning. *(Video 87)*

**Q5 - MC:** What is the difference between an assumption and a fact in the context of the assumption log?
A) There is no difference
B) An assumption is perceived to be true but unverified; a fact has been confirmed
C) An assumption is always negative; a fact is always positive
D) Assumptions are documented; facts are not
**A:** B) Assumptions are perceived to be true and may constrain the project if proven false. Facts have been verified. The assumption log tracks these throughout the project. *(Video 87)*

**Q6 - [SCENARIO]:** A contractor signs a service level agreement with your company to maintain internet at 99% availability. This agreement becomes an input to which process?
A) Collect Requirements
B) Develop Project Charter
C) Plan Procurements
D) Identify Stakeholders
**A:** B) Agreements (contracts, SLAs, letters of intent) are inputs to Develop Project Charter because they initiate projects for the performing organization. *(Video 87)*

## Section 8: The 49 Processes — Planning

### Develop Project Management Plan (Video 89)

**Q1 - MC:** The project management plan is BEST described as:
A) A single document listing the project schedule
B) A comprehensive document outlining the basis of all work and how the work will be done
C) The project charter with more detail added
D) A list of all project risks
**A:** B) The PM plan outlines what work will be done (baselines) and how to do it (management plans). It contains 18 components: 14 subsidiary plans and 4 baselines. *(Video 89)*

**Q2 - [SCENARIO]:** A communication issue arises on your project. What should you do FIRST?
A) Call an emergency meeting with all stakeholders
B) Escalate to the sponsor
C) Consult the communication management plan
D) Send an email to the team
**A:** C) The first thing a PM should do when a problem arises is consult the relevant management plan. The communication management plan has the steps to handle communication issues. *(Video 89)*

**Q3 - MC:** What is the difference between a management plan and a baseline?
A) They are the same thing
B) A management plan is a how-to document; a baseline defines what the work is
C) A baseline is a how-to document; a management plan defines what the work is
D) Management plans are optional; baselines are mandatory
**A:** B) Management plans = how-to documents (how to manage scope, cost, schedule, etc.). Baselines = what the work is (scope baseline, cost baseline, schedule baseline). *(Video 89)*

**Q4 - [SCENARIO]:** A team member wants to change the project scope. What must happen before any change is made to the approved project management plan?
A) The PM can approve the change directly
B) A change request must be generated and approved by the CCB or sponsor
C) The team can implement it if it improves the project
D) Changes to the plan are not allowed
**A:** B) Once the PM plan is approved, only an approved change request (via the change control board or sponsor) can modify it. *(Video 89)*

**Q5 - MC:** The project management plan contains how many baselines?
A) 2

### PERT Calculations (Video 98)

**Q1 - MC:** The PERT formula is:
A) (O + R + P) / 3
B) (O + 4R + P) / 6
C) (O + P) / 2
D) (4O + R + P) / 6
**A:** B) (Optimistic + 4 × Realistic + Pessimistic) / 6. The realistic value is weighted 4x because it's the most likely outcome. *(Video 98)*

**Q2 - [CALCULATION]:** A painter gives three estimates: best case = 2 days, most likely = 4 days, worst case = 12 days. What is the PERT estimate?
A) 4 days
B) 5 days
C) 6 days
D) 7 days
**A:** B) 5 days. (2 + 4×4 + 12) / 6 = (2 + 16 + 12) / 6 = 30 / 6 = 5. *(Video 98)*

**Q3 - MC:** Standard deviation in PERT gives you:
A) The most likely duration
B) The range of the estimate (plus or minus)
C) The total project duration
D) The cost of the activity
**A:** B) Standard deviation = (P - O) / 6. It provides the range: the PERT estimate ± standard deviation. *(Video 98)*

**Q4 - [CALCULATION]:** Given O=2, R=4, P=12, what is the standard deviation?
A) 1.0
B) 1.7
C) 2.0
D) 3.3
**A:** B) 1.7. (12 - 2) / 6 = 10 / 6 = 1.67 ≈ 1.7. This means the estimate of 5 days is ±1.7 days. *(Video 98)*

**Q5 - MC:** The triangular distribution formula differs from PERT because:
A) It weights the realistic value 4x
B) It's a simple average: (O + R + P) / 3 — no weighting
C) It only uses two values
D) It calculates standard deviation
**A:** B) Triangular distribution = (O + R + P) / 3. Unlike PERT, it treats all three values equally (non-weighted average). *(Video 98)*

**Q6 - [CALCULATION]:** Given O=10, R=15, P=40, what is the PERT estimate?
A) 15.0
B) 17.5
C) 18.3
D) 21.7
**A:** C) 18.3. (10 + 4×15 + 40) / 6 = (10 + 60 + 40) / 6 = 110 / 6 = 18.3. *(Video 98)*

### Critical Path Method — CPM (Videos 100-109)

**Q1 - MC:** The critical path is defined as:
A) The path with the most activities
B) The shortest path through the network diagram
C) The longest path through the network diagram, determining the minimum project duration
D) The path with the most resources assigned
**A:** C) The critical path is the longest path from start to finish. It determines the minimum project duration — the project cannot finish faster than the critical path allows. *(Video 102)*

**Q2 - MC:** Float (also called slack or total float) is:
A) The amount of time you can delay an activity without delaying the next activity
B) The amount of time you can delay an activity without delaying the project end date
C) Extra budget reserved for risks
D) The time between two milestones
**A:** B) Float/slack/total float = the amount of time an activity can be delayed without delaying the project end date. Activities on the critical path have zero float. *(Video 102)*

**Q3 - [SCENARIO]:** Your network diagram has two paths: Path 1 (A-B-C-E) = 11 days and Path 2 (A-B-D-E) = 10 days. Activity D has a float of:
A) 0 days
B) 1 day
C) 2 days
D) 10 days
**A:** B) 1 day. Path 2 is 1 day shorter than the critical path (11-10=1). Activity D can be delayed by 1 day without affecting the project end date. *(Video 102)*

**Q4 - [SCENARIO]:** Activity B is on the critical path and is estimated at 2 days. If B takes 3 days instead, what happens?
A) Nothing — there is buffer time
B) The project end date is extended by 1 day
C) Only the next activity is delayed
D) The schedule is automatically compressed
**A:** B) The project is extended by 1 day. Any delay to a critical path activity directly delays the project end date by the same amount — there is no float on critical path activities. *(Video 102)*

**Q5 - MC:** The forward pass through a network diagram calculates:
A) Late start and late finish for each activity
B) Early start and early finish for each activity
C) Float for each activity
D) The project budget
**A:** B) The forward pass calculates early start (ES) and early finish (EF) for every activity, starting from day 1. *(Video 104)*

**Q6 - MC:** The formula for early finish (EF) in the forward pass is:
A) EF = ES + Duration
B) EF = ES + Duration - 1
C) EF = ES - Duration + 1
D) EF = LF - Duration
**A:** B) EF = ES + Duration - 1. You subtract 1 because you start working on the ES day itself (it counts as a full day of work). *(Video 104)*

**Q7 - [SCENARIO]:** During a forward pass, activities C (EF=9) and D (EF=8) both converge into activity E. What is E's early start?
A) 8
B) 9
C) 10
D) 11
**A:** C) 10. At a path convergence going forward, E must wait for the biggest predecessor to finish. The biggest EF is 9 (from C), so E starts the next day: 9+1=10. *(Video 104)*

**Q8 - MC:** The backward pass through a network diagram calculates:
A) Early start and early finish
B) Late start and late finish
C) The critical path duration
D) Free float only
**A:** B) The backward pass calculates late start (LS) and late finish (LF), starting from the critical path end date and working backwards. *(Video 105)*

**Q9 - MC:** The formula for late start (LS) in the backward pass is:
A) LS = LF + Duration - 1
B) LS = LF - Duration
C) LS = LF - Duration + 1
D) LS = ES - Duration
**A:** C) LS = LF - Duration + 1. This is the inverse of the forward pass formula. *(Video 105)*

**Q10 - [SCENARIO]:** During a backward pass, activities C (LS=6) and D (LS=7) both diverge from activity B. What is B's late finish?
A) 5
B) 6
C) 7
D) 8
**A:** A) 5. At a path convergence going backwards, decrease the smallest late start by 1. The smallest LS is 6 (from C), so B's LF = 6-1 = 5. Forward: increment the biggest. Backward: decrease the smallest. *(Video 105)*

**Q11 - MC:** How do you calculate total float for an activity?
A) ES - LS or EF - LF
B) LS - ES or LF - EF
C) ES + EF - LS - LF
D) Duration - ES
**A:** B) Total float = LS - ES, or equivalently LF - EF. Both give the same result. Quick tip: bottom numbers minus top numbers, left or right side. *(Video 103)*

**Q12 - MC:** How do you know an activity is on the critical path by looking at its ES, EF, LS, and LF values?
A) ES is always 1
B) ES = LS and EF = LF (top numbers equal bottom numbers, float = 0)
C) The activity has the longest duration
D) The activity has the most resources
**A:** B) When ES = LS and EF = LF, the float is zero — the activity is on the critical path. There is no room for delay. *(Video 105)*

**Q13 - MC:** What is the difference between total float and free float?
A) They are the same thing
B) Total float = delay without delaying the project; free float = delay without delaying the NEXT activity
C) Free float = delay without delaying the project; total float = delay without delaying the next activity
D) Total float applies to critical path activities only
**A:** B) Total float measures delay before the project is impacted. Free float measures delay before the next activity is impacted. Free float ≤ total float. *(Video 106)*

**Q14 - MC:** The formula for free float is:
A) LS - ES
B) LF - EF
C) ES(next activity) - EF(current activity) - 1
D) LF(next activity) - EF(current activity)
**A:** C) Free float = ES of the next activity - EF of the current activity - 1. *(Video 106)*

**Q15 - [SCENARIO]:** On a complex network diagram with 8+ paths, what is the fastest way to identify the critical path?
A) Calculate all paths and find the longest
B) Do the forward and backward pass — activities where top numbers equal bottom numbers (zero float) are on the critical path
C) Ask the team which activities are most important
D) Use only the backward pass
**A:** B) For complex diagrams, doing forward + backward passes is faster than listing all paths. Activities with zero float (ES=LS, EF=LF) are on the critical path. *(Video 109)*
